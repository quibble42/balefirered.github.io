What is Balefire?

Balefire gives you services, tech, and knowledge to make your company the best version of itself.
We specialize in bringing companies into the 21st century. Website design, inventory management, data-based market determination…You name it, we can do it.
Balefire started in 2018 when John Mele, the founder, wanted to take skills he learned at a logistics position to a wider clientele. His goal is to give companies ‘room to breathe’ by keeping the age-old methods that have worked for years but making them easier to keep up with as the company grows.
If you run a marathon, you don’t run them in 20-year old boots. When you get new shoes, you run faster, you run better, and it doesn't take as much out of you. Balefire can be your company's new shoes.

What Services Do We Offer?

  1. Keep what works—Just faster.

    There’s no such thing as failure when it comes to your business. There’s a reason you’ve been doing it that way for years—it works. We automate it, optimize it, and overall make your life better, while still keeping the methods the same.

  2.  Your workflow—and no-one else’s

    “Agile”…“Scrum”…“The newest in workflow technology”… One size fits all is just another way to say “one way fits you just fine”. We don’t expect “just fine” coffee, or “just fine” professional suits, and your customers don’t want “just fine” from you.
    We create a workflow that works for YOU, and is for perfect for you. Sure, Agile Workflow might increase your efficiency by 70%–But, something we make for you can bring you to 100%.

  3. UX design and website optimization

    Is your website just a website or is it a tool?

    • We optimize your website to improve loading time (sometimes reducing space up to 80%)
    • We give your website purpose. Turn customers looking for information into clients.
    • We maintain your brand with imagery that will stay with the customer long after they visit your site.


Our Case Studies

  • CLW Distributors, Inc.

    At CLW, reports took an entire person to do each week. After automation, those reports were done by that same person before lunch every Monday.
    In e-commerce, an employee averaged 15 hours each week handling orders that came in through the website. After integration, that 15 hours turns into zero.
    Over the course of one year, this adds up to 2704 hours, or savings of over $58,000 (evaluated for two employees each making 45k yearly).
    Instead of laying off employees, this allowed for those employees to make more sales calls, update more on the website, and ultimately, make more sales. Scaleable technologies means that you don't need employees doing busy work.
